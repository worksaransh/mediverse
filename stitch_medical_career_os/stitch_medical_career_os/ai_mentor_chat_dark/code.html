Can you explain the current first-line management for a 65-year-old male presenting with acute decompensated heart failure and a history of reduced ejection fraction?

psychology Mentor Analysis

Management of acute decompensated heart failure (ADHF) focuses on optimizing hemodynamic stability and volume status. For a patient with HFrEF, the initial approach typically involves:

  • Diuretic Therapy: Intravenous loop diuretics (e.g., Furosemide) are the cornerstone. If the patient is already on oral diuretics, the IV dose should be 2.5 times the oral dose.
  • Vasodilators: Consider nitroglycerin or nitroprusside if the patient is hypertensive, to reduce afterload.
  • Oxygenation: Supplemental O2 or non-invasive positive pressure ventilation (NIPPV) if SpO2 < 90% or in respiratory distress.
